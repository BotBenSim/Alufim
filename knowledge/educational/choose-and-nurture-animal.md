# Choose and nurture your own animal

## Decision

The child picks a profile and an animal, then raises that specific animal. XP and evolution
are stored per animal, and the child can switch games freely without losing progress. Data in
[`web/data/characters.ts`](../../web/data/characters.ts).

## Why it helps the child

Intrinsic motivation grows from autonomy (I chose this), competence (I can see it growing),
and relatedness (I care about it). A chosen, nurtured animal delivers all three, turning
practice into caring for "my" animal.

## How it works

Progress is keyed to the chosen animal, so XP and forms accumulate on that bond. Games are
just XP sources, so switching game mid-run keeps the same animal and progress.

## Watch-outs

- Locking animals behind grind or payment erodes autonomy.
- Sharing or resetting progress across animals would break the attachment and the sense of
  earned competence.
